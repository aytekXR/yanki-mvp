Yankı — Brand Guidelines

Brand identity & design guidelines

Yankı measures how AI answer engines talk about a brand — and shows every raw answer behind the score. This document defines the brand: strategy, logo, color, type, voice, and rules of use.

v1.0 July 2026 yankı — "echo" (Turkish)
01

Brand strategy

Positioning

The transparent GEO measurement tool. Where incumbents sell a black-box score at enterprise prices, Yankı publishes its methodology, links every score to the raw AI answers behind it, and prices so an agency can run it across thirty clients.

Value proposition

URL in, GEO score out in ~10 minutes — with every raw answer one click away. Know exactly how often ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity mention your brand, and see the evidence yourself.

Mission

Make AI visibility measurable, explainable, and affordable for every brand — not just the ones with enterprise budgets.

Vision

As AI answers replace search results, Yankı becomes the standard instrument for measuring a brand's echo across generative engines — in every market and every language, starting with Turkish as a first-class citizen.

Personality

The knowledgeable, honest analyst. Calm, precise, quietly confident. Shows numbers instead of claiming results. Never hypey, never vague.

Core values

Show your work. Own the limits. Price fairly. Keep it simple enough for a junior to read. Speak the customer's language — literally.

Tone of voice

Plain, specific, evidence-first. "You appear in 9 of 20 answers" — never "Great visibility!" No dark patterns, no fake urgency.

Target audience

1 — the multiplier

Agency owner

15–30 clients. Needs client-ready numbers without per-domain fees. If it works across ten clients, it works.

2 — the reporter

In-house SEO lead

The VP keeps asking "how do we look in ChatGPT?" Needs one score, a trend, and screenshots for a deck.

3 — the discoverer

Founder

Just learned an AI recommends a competitor. Wants a free check first, a cheap plan second, in their own language.

All three buy self-serve — no sales calls, no demos. The brand must earn trust on the page alone.

Competitive positioning

Against Semrush-class incumbents: they are expensive at scale, opaque about how scores are derived, thin for small brands, and English-only. Yankı wins on transparency (published methodology, raw answers visible), agency-scalable pricing, and native Turkish. The visual identity reinforces this: instrument-like restraint against their marketing-heavy orange and purple.

Naming

Yankı is Turkish for echo — what an AI engine says about you is your brand's echo. The name encodes both the product (measuring the echo) and the differentiator (Turkish-first). The dotless ı is kept everywhere; it is a feature, not a typo. Written Yankı in prose, yankı in the wordmark.

Taglines

"See how AI answers talk about your brand." — primary, product

"Your brand has an echo. Measure it." — campaign

"Every score, shown with its work." — trust / methodology

02

Logo system

The mark is a ping and its echo: a solid dot emitting three arcs that thin as they travel — a signal returning. It is born from the dotless ı that ends the wordmark. Three arcs, always; widths 6 / 5 / 4 at base scale.

Primary — horizontal

Default everywhere: product header, website, documents. Symbol in Echo Teal, wordmark in Ink.

Secondary — vertical & symbol

Vertical for square placements. Symbol alone only once the name is established in context (app icon, favicon, avatars).

Clearspace & minimum size

Clearspace = the dot's diameter on all sides. Minimum width: 96px horizontal lockup, 20px symbol.

One accent, restrained neutrals, semantic status colors. Deep petrol ink and echo teal replace the previous generic indigo — distinctive against incumbents, still calm and technical. Always use tokens in code, never raw hexes.

Accessibility

  • Echo Teal on white: 5.0:1 — passes AA for text and UI. White text on Echo Teal passes for bold/large and buttons.
  • Text-on-soft variants (#116038 / #8A4B08 / #9E332E) exist because base status hues fail 4.5:1 on their own soft fills — always use them for text or glyphs on soft backgrounds.
  • Signal #3BD1B5 is dark-surface only; it fails contrast on white.
  • Score bands are never color-only — always paired with the numeric percentage and a label.

05

Visual language

Shapes & motifs

Dots, arcs, and concentric rings — always echoes of the mark. No abstract blobs, no isometric illustration.

Icons

1.5px stroke, rounded caps and joins, 24px grid (Lucide-compatible). Never filled, never two-tone.

Data over decoration

Real numbers, gauges, and mono snippets are the imagery. No stock photos, no mascots, no gradient meshes.

Radius

12px cards · 8px inputs/panels · 6px buttons · full for badges and the gauge track.

Shadow & borders

Hairline borders first; at most a soft shadow (0 1px 2px rgba(11,29,38,0.06)) on raised cards. On dark, borders only.

Grid & spacing

4px base scale (8 / 12 / 16 / 24 / 32 / 48 / 64). Content max-width 896px in product, 1080px on marketing. Gutter 16px mobile / 32px desktop.

08

Do & don't

Do

  • Keep the dotless ı in every rendering of the name.
  • Use one accent per surface; let whitespace carry the layout.
  • Pair every score or status color with a number or label.
  • Set raw data (JSON, snippets, scores) in IBM Plex Mono.
  • Use design tokens in code — never raw hexes in components.
  • State limits plainly wherever a score is shown.

Don't

  • Don't write "Yanki" with a dotted i in brand surfaces (ASCII contexts like URLs and code are the exception).
  • Don't add arcs, rotate the mark, or change arc count/weights.
  • Don't use Signal teal on light backgrounds — it fails contrast.
  • Don't use gradients in the product UI; they are marketing-only.
  • Don't use hype copy, emoji, exclamation points, or fake urgency.
  • Don't hide data: every score links to the answers behind it.
